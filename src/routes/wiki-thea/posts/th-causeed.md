The Causeed occupies a paramount position in the theological framework of hominid species across Thea. Revered as the primal and uncaused cause, Causeed represents the origin of existence and is integral to the understanding of cosmic causality in Thean beliefs.

##### religious significance

Causeed is conceptualized as the ultimate source of all existence, predating and initiating all cosmic phenomena. In the Bividan doctrine, Causeed is placed before Kim'asta in the causality loops, signifying its precedence and supremacy in the order of divine entities.

Causeed is a symbol of the beginning, the fundamental source from which all things in the Thea galaxy emanate. Different hominid species and cultures within Thea have their unique interpretations and representations of Causeed, reflecting the entity's profound and multifaceted nature. Causeed's role as the uncaused cause presents intriguing philosophical questions about the nature of existence and the origin of the universe in Thean thought. The concept of Causeed is a subject of extensive mystical and metaphysical studies among Thean scholars, philosophers, and theologians.

##### depiction

While specific depictions of Causeed vary, it is represented in various forms of religious art, scripture, and architecture across Thea. As a central divine figure, Causeed is invoked in significant religious ceremonies and prayers, especially those concerning creation, existence, and the understanding of the universe.

Causeed, as the uncaused cause, stands at the apex of Thean theological and philosophical understanding. This entity's role as the primordial source of all existence imbues it with an aura of mystery and reverence. In the diverse map of Thean belief systems, Causeed's presence as a fundamental divine figure highlights the complex and rich nature of cosmic and existential exploration in the Thea galaxy.
