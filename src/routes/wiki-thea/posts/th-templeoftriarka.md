The Temple of Triarka, dedicated to the revered maharishi Triarka, is a significant spiritual and architectural landmark in Thea. This temple, particularly the largest one in Kadorra, is not only a place of worship but also a symbol of the profound impact Maharishi Triarka has had on the spiritual and cultural life of the galaxy.

The design of the Temple of Triarka is inspired by the essence of fire, a central element in maharishi Triarka's teachings. The architecture features fluid, flame-like structures that seem to reach towards the sky, symbolizing the aspiration for enlightenment and spiritual upliftment. The temple's exterior is adorned with golden and red hues, mirroring the colors of a blazing fire.

The structure is designed to resemble the wisps of an open flame, creating a dynamic and awe-inspiring visual effect. This design not only reflects the spiritual fervor associated with Triarka's teachings but also represents the transformative power of fire – symbolizing purification, renewal, and change.

Inside, the temple is equally majestic, with a large central chamber where a statue of Maharishi Triarka sits in a meditative pose. This chamber is designed to focus energy and facilitate meditation and introspection. The walls are lined with intricate carvings and murals depicting scenes from Triarka's life and his various reincarnations, providing devotees with visual narratives of his teachings.

The temple also features several smaller chambers used for meditation, study, and the practice of various rituals integral to Triarka's teachings. These spaces are designed to foster a sense of peace and spiritual connection.
