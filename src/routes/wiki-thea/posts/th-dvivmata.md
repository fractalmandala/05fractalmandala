D'vi V'mata is a unique goddess revered in Ahurunian tradition on Kadorra. As a deity of mystic world-sheets and ascension mind-states, she represents a specific aspect of spiritual and metaphysical beliefs that are exclusive to Ahurunian culture.

##### religious significance

Her worship is not found in other regions of Kadorra, indicating a unique cultural and spiritual identity within the Ahurunian tradition. D'vi V'mata embodies the quest for spiritual enlightenment and the exploration of mystical realms. Her followers are primarily located in the region of Ahurun, where she holds significant religious importance. Worship of D'vi V'mata involves practices and rituals aimed at achieving spiritual ascension and connection with mystic world-sheets.

D'vi V'mata is associated with otherworldly realms and dimensions, symbolizing the connection between the material world and higher planes of existence. She is believed to guide and influence the journey of the atman towards higher states of consciousness and spiritual ascension.

##### depiction

Her influence extends to the cultural expressions of the Ahurunian people, inspiring artistic works and literature that reflect her attributes and teachings.

D'vi V'mata stands as a distinctive figure in the theological landscape of Kadorra, specifically within the Ahurunian tradition. Her association with mystic world-sheets and ascension mind-states highlights the diverse and rich spiritual beliefs present in Thea.
