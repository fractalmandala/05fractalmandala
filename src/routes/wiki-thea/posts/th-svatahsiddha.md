The Svatahsiddhas are revered entities in the rich tapestry of Thean theology, occupying a unique and profound place in the spiritual landscape of the galaxy. As second-order divinities, they are considered more approachable and interactive with the material world than the higher-order deities, playing a direct role in the affairs of the various hominid species and other sentient beings in Thea.

Unlike the distant and impersonal Ritakartas, Svatahsiddhas are seen as actively participating in the cosmic drama. They are depicted as having both transcendent and immanent qualities, capable of manifesting in the physical world and influencing events, cultures, and individuals. Each Svatahsiddha is unique in appearance and nature, often represented in Thean art and literature in various forms that symbolize their attributes and domains of influence.

Svatahsiddhas are believed to act as intermediaries between the divine and the material realms. They are the custodians of cosmic order and balance, intervening directly when this balance is threatened. They are often invoked in times of crisis, change, and when guidance is needed.

Their roles extend to being patrons of arts, sciences, and other aspects of civilization. Different Svatahsiddhas are associated with different aspects of life and the universe, such as creation, knowledge, justice, or the natural elements.

##### culture and media
In Thean society, Svatahsiddhas are venerated through various rituals, ceremonies, and practices. Their temples are places of pilgrimage and spiritual convergence, attracting devotees from across the galaxy. Festivals dedicated to them are marked by grand celebrations, where stories of their deeds and teachings are shared.

The teachings and parables attributed to the Svatahsiddhas form a significant part of the moral and ethical education in Thean cultures, guiding individuals in their personal and communal lives. The principles and virtues espoused by the Svatahsiddhas have a profound impact on Thean governance and societal norms. Leaders and rulers often seek the blessings and guidance of Svatahsiddhas, striving to align their policies with the divine order upheld by these entities.

The Svatahsiddhas also serve as symbols of unity and common purpose among the diverse species of Thea, transcending individual beliefs and cultural differences.
