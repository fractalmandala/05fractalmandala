Creator Ma is a universally acknowledged mother goddess. Her presence is felt across various cultures and species within Thea, symbolizing a shared reverence for the feminine divine and the concept of creation.

##### religious significance

As a mother goddess, Creator Ma is seen as the source of life and existence, a central figure in creation myths and cosmologies. Her worship underscores the importance of the feminine aspect of divinity in Thean spirituality. Creator Ma's worship transcends planetary and species boundaries, making her one of the few universally venerated entities in Thea, ie- by both hominid and reptilian species. Ceremonies dedicated to Creator Ma involve offerings and rituals that celebrate life, fertility, and creation.

##### depiction

Despite cultural and species diversity, Creator Ma is consistently depicted as a mother figure, embodying nurturing, creation, and the genesis of life. Vaktuki Tritha affirmed that the various forms worshipped across cultures are indeed depictions of the same Creator Ma, unifying her significance across the galaxy.

##### culture and media

Her universal acceptance makes Creator Ma a unifying figure in the otherwise diverse theological landscape of Thea. The depiction of Creator Ma influences a wide range of artistic expressions, from temple architecture to ritualistic performances.

Creator Ma is often associated with spiritual wisdom, offering guidance on matters of creation, life, and moral integrity. Thean scholars and theologians continually explore and debate her role in the cosmic order, contributing to a rich tradition of spiritual and philosophical discourse.

Creator Ma's position as a mother goddess in Thean theology represents a profound aspect of spiritual life across Thea. Her universal reverence bridges cultural and species differences, embodying a shared acknowledgment of the feminine divine and its vital role in the genesis and nurturing of life. As a symbol of creation and unity, Creator Ma continues to inspire and guide a multitude of species in their spiritual and existential journeys.
