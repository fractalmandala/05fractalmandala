The Temples of Asti, erected in honor of the Asti, a world-sheet represented as three divinities in the traditions of Kadorra and the Vaunaki, are among the most revered and architecturally significant structures in Thea. Each temple, dedicated to one of the three states of Asti - Being, Non-Being, and Becoming - symbolizes the profound philosophical and spiritual concepts central to Thean theology.

The design of each Temple of Asti is a marvel of architectural achievement, reflecting the profound nature of the divinities they honor.

**Temple of Being:** This temple is characterized by its imposing structure, featuring grand pillars and a vast central dome, symbolizing the fullness and presence of being. The interior is adorned with intricate mosaics and sculptures that depict the vibrancy and richness of existence.

**Temple of Non-Being:** In contrast, the Temple of Non-Being is designed with an emphasis on space and minimalism. It features open courtyards and expansive halls that lead to a central void, representing the concept of non-existence or the void before creation. The use of reflective surfaces and water elements creates a serene and contemplative environment.

**Temple of Becoming:** The Temple of Becoming is a dynamic structure, incorporating elements of both the other temples. It is characterized by a spiraling design, with pathways leading visitors through various stages of ascent, symbolizing the process of becoming. The walls are adorned with depictions of transformation and evolution, celebrating the continuous cycle of change and growth.

##### culture and media
The Temples of Asti are not just places of worship but are central to the cultural and intellectual life of Thea. They serve as centers for philosophical discourse, art exhibitions, and spiritual retreats. Festivals and ceremonies held at these temples attract participants from various species, fostering a sense of universal connection and understanding.

The rituals performed in these temples are elaborate and symbolic, designed to align participants with the cosmic forces represented by the Asti. These rituals often involve meditation, chanting, and offerings, and are led by priests and priestesses who are deeply knowledgeable about the Asti and their significance.

Given their importance, the Temples of Asti are meticulously maintained and preserved. Access to these temples is generally open to all, reflecting the inclusive nature of Thean spirituality. However, certain areas, especially those used for specific rituals, might be restricted to preserve their sanctity.
