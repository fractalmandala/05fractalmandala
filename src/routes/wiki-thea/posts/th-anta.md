The Anta, an enigmatic and ancient species, are the ancestors of the Prakas. This mysterious race is central to the theologies and eschatologies of the hominid species that inhabit Thea. Their legend is intertwined with forebodings of invasion and conflict, casting a long shadow over the history and future of the galaxy. The Anta, as ancestral beings to the Prakas, occupy a pivotal position in the history and future of the Thea galaxy. Their anticipated return, shrouded in mystery and prophecy, continues to influence the cultural, religious, and strategic thinking of the many species that call Thea home. As a key element in the intricate tapestry of Thean lore, the Anta remain a source of fascination, fear, and reverence.

##### religious significance

Thean teleology, particularly the teachings of Vaktuki Tritha, prophesizes an impending invasion by the Anta. This event is seen as an inevitable, cataclysmic confrontation that will shape the destiny of Thea. The final years of the Tritha Twins were dedicated to preparing for the Antas' arrival. Their strategies and defenses are part of the military and cultural fabric of Thea.

In Thean mythology, the Anta represent a primal force, often associated with darkness and the unknown. Their anticipated return is a subject of various tales and artistic expressions across the galaxy. They are integral to the eschatological beliefs in Thea. Their role in the cosmic cycle of creation, destruction, and rebirth is a topic of deep theological debate and interpretation.

##### depiction

There is little concrete knowledge about the physical appearance or capabilities of the Anta. This lack of information fuels speculation and myth-making, adding to their mystique. Thean scholars and theologians continually study and debate the role of the Anta in the grand cosmic design, often arriving at conflicting theories and interpretations.
