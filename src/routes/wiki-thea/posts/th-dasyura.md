Dasyura are a class of anti-divine beings originating from non-material world-sheets. They are universally considered malevolent and immoral, contrasting with other entities like An'agga who are viewed as amoral.

##### religious significance

Dasyura are portrayed as embodying malevolence and immorality, representing the darker aspects of Thean cosmology. While both are malevolent, Dasyura are distinct in their active pursuit of immoral objectives, as opposed to An'agga's amoral nature. Unlike other divine or anti-divine beings, Dasyura are not objects of worship but are instead acknowledged in rituals and practices aimed at protection from evil. Various Thean cultures have developed specific rituals and practices to guard against the influence or interference of Dasyura.

##### culture and media

Dasyura feature prominently in the myths and legends of various Thean cultures, often serving as antagonists in cosmic narratives. They are symbolic of the concept of evil in Thean cultures, embodying the challenges that moral beings must confront.

The concept of Dasyura influences moral and ethical teachings across Thea, serving as a cautionary example of the paths not to follow. Dasyura often feature in Thean art and literature, symbolizing the eternal struggle between good and evil.

Dasyura, as malevolent anti-divine entities, play a significant role in shaping the theological and moral landscape of Thea. Their presence in myth and culture serves as a reminder of the darker forces at play in the universe, and the ongoing struggle between morality and immorality. As symbols of evil and immorality, they continue to influence a wide range of cultural expressions, spiritual beliefs, and ethical discourses throughout Thea.
