**Phylum:** Basidiomycota
**Class:** Agaricomycetes
**Order:** Myceliales
**Family:** Antamyceliaceae
**Genus:** Antamycelium

Mycelis Anta is an enigmatic and lesser-known species within the mycelis genus, primarily known through anecdotal reports from explorers and references in Thean traditions. Unlike its relative, mycelis adimycelis, mycelis anta's exact origin or native planet remains a mystery, adding to its allure and mystique in galactic biodiversity studies.

Mycelis anta is reported to resemble the common mycelis species in its fundamental structure but diverges significantly in size and coloration. It is said to have a more intimidating appearance, with larger, darker caps that are often described as having a sinister quality. Known for its alleged carnivorous and aggressive nature, mycelis anta is feared as a flesh-eating species. This characteristic is unique and unheard of in other mycelis species, marking it as an outlier and a subject of both fear and fascination.

It has attained a somewhat mythical status, often featured in tales of perilous explorations and as a symbol of nature's hidden and dangerous aspects. The species represents a tantalizing mystery for xenobiologists, who are eager to study its unique characteristics and understand its place in the broader context of extraterrestrial life forms.
