The Urka, an extinct species native to Thea, holds a significant place in the galaxy's ecological and historical narrative. Believed to be either a direct ancestor or a close relative of the Ka'sinh species, the Urka's existence and eventual extinction provide valuable insights into the evolutionary pathways and environmental shifts on Thean planets.

##### description and habitat
The Urka was a large, formidable creature, characterized by its robust build and distinctive features. It possessed a thick, scaled hide, which provided both protection and camouflage in its natural habitat. The Urka had a powerful jawline with sharp teeth, indicative of its carnivorous diet. Its limbs were muscular and strong, suggesting a capacity for swift movement and agility.

One of the most striking features of the Urka was its elongated snout, which was lined with sensory receptors, giving it an acute sense of smell and environmental awareness. Its eyes were large and perceptive, adapted for both diurnal and nocturnal activities.

Urka primarily inhabited forested and mountainous regions, where it held the position of an apex predator. Its diet consisted mainly of smaller fauna, and it was known for its territorial nature. The Urka was a solitary creature, coming together with others of its kind only during mating seasons.

The species demonstrated a high level of adaptability, able to thrive in various environmental conditions. This adaptability, however, was eventually challenged by drastic ecological changes and possibly by competition from emerging species.
