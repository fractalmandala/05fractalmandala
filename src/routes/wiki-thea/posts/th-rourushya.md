The Rourushya are one of the six hominid species inhabiting the galaxy of Thea, known for their tragic history and resilience. Originating from a now devastated homeworld, Madura, the Rourushya have faced immense challenges and adversities, notably during the Fourth Great War, but have continued to persevere and adapt in the face of near extinction.

Rourushyas are characterized by their distinct physical features, which include elongated limbs and a generally taller stature compared to other hominid species of Thea. Their skin tones vary widely, often reflecting the diverse ecological zones of their native planet. A unique aspect of Rourushya physiology is their adaptability to different environmental conditions, a trait that has been crucial for their survival post-displacement.

Before the devastation at their homeworld, Rourushya society was known for its rich cultural heritage, emphasizing communal living and a deep connection to their natural environment. They were known for their artistic expressions, especially in the form of music and dance, which were integral parts of their daily life and spiritual practices.

Post-displacement, Rourushya culture underwent significant changes, as they adapted to life as refugees on Kadorra. While they have strived to preserve their traditions, the influence of Kadorran culture is evident in the evolution of their societal norms and practices.

##### history
The Rourushya homeworld was rendered inhabitable in the Fourth Great War. The use of a Praka Terraformer rendered their planet uninhabitable, leading to massive casualties and forcing the survivors to flee. This event marked a turning point in Rourushya history, transitioning them from a thriving civilization to a displaced community seeking refuge.

The majority of surviving Rourushyas found asylum in the region of Askini on Kadorra, where they were integrated into the local society. Despite the challenges of adapting to a new world and the trauma of losing their home, the Rourushyas have shown remarkable resilience and a strong will to rebuild their community.
