The First Great War holds a unique and somber place in Thean history, particularly within the traditions of Kadorra. It is distinct for being the only great war fought entirely among Kadorrans and is remembered for its catastrophic consequences, including the infamous nuking of Ahurun. Unlike subsequent Great Wars involving external threats, this war was an internal conflict, reflecting deep divisions within the Manushyas of Kadorra.

##### history

Unlike subsequent Great Wars involving external threats, this war was an internal conflict, reflecting deep divisions within Kadorran society. It was characterized by the use of advanced, destructive technologies, notably nuclear weapons, which led to unprecedented devastation and drew the attention of the antagonist Dirgham species.

A defining and tragic event of the war was the nuking of Ahurun, one of Kadorra's major megapolises, leading to immense loss of life and a long-lasting impact on the planet. The First Great War saw significant tactical and strategic developments, setting a precedent for future conflicts in Thean history.

The aftermath of the war plunged Kadorra into a prolonged period of darkness, both literally and metaphorically, deeply affecting its cultural, social, and environmental landscapes. The ecological consequences were severe, with lasting effects on the planet's environment and biodiversity.

##### culture and media

The First Great War is ingrained in the collective memory of Kadorrans as a period of tragedy and a cautionary tale about the dangers of unchecked technological advancement and internal strife. The war's impact extends to Kadorran art, literature, and historical narratives, often depicted as a pivotal moment of transformation and reflection.
