The vaubudha-gaja, a remarkable species native to the Vaunaki homeworld. This species, known for its unique characteristics and cultural significance, plays an integral role in the ecosystems of its native planet and in the lives of the Vaunaki people.

##### description and habitat
Vaubudha-gajas are large, herbivorous creatures, distinguished by their impressive size and gentle nature. They possess a thick, leathery skin, which can range in color from a deep gray to a lighter shade of brown. One of the most striking features of the vaubudha-gaja is its elongated tusks, which are not only a tool for foraging and defense but also a symbol of age and wisdom in Vaunaki culture.

These creatures have a robust, muscular build, with strong legs capable of carrying their significant weight. Their eyes are small compared to their size, but they have a keen sense of hearing and smell, which helps them navigate their environment and communicate with one another. Vaubudha-gajas primarily inhabit the grasslands and forested regions of the Vaunaki homeworld, Ormaunk. 

##### behavior
They are social animals, living in herds that can range from a few individuals to several dozen. Their diet consists mainly of grass, leaves, and fruits, and they are known to travel great distances to find food and water.

One of the most remarkable aspects of vaubudha-gaja behavior is their symbiotic relationship with the Vaunaki nomad bands. These creatures have been partly domesticated and are often found following the nomadic tribes, contributing to the tribe's livelihood through their strength and utility.
