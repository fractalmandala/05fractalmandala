The Adiputras are a revered group of divine beings in the lore of Thea's hominid species. They are regarded as the first 'mind-born' or self-created entities, emerging from the cosmic consciousness. In the intricate hierarchy of divinities detailed by Vaktuki Tritha, the Adiputras are positioned at a high level, only surpassed by primary creator deities.

##### religious significance

The Adiputras hold a crucial place in Thean spirituality and mythology. They are believed to be the direct progenitors of knowledge, wisdom, and spiritual enlightenment. Each Adiputra embodies specific cosmic principles or aspects of existence, such as creation, destruction, preservation, time, and knowledge.

In the traditions and rituals of the Thean hominid species, the Adiputras are frequently invoked for guidance, wisdom, and the attainment of higher knowledge. Their teachings and philosophies are integral to various forms of worship and spiritual practice. In the broader cosmic narrative of Thea, the Adiputras play a pivotal role in the ongoing cycle of creation and dissolution. They are seen as custodians of cosmic order and balance, intervening in the affairs of the universe to maintain harmony and prevent chaos. The lore often describes interactions between the Adiputras and other divine or semi-divine beings, highlighting their role in guiding and influencing the course of cosmic events and the evolution of life across the galaxy.

##### depiction

The Adiputras are a popular subject in Thean art, literature, and sacred texts. They are often depicted in a state of deep contemplation or engaged in acts of imparting wisdom to the lesser divinities or mortal beings. While universally acknowledged across Thean cultures, the perception and specific attributes of the Adiputras can vary. Some cultures emphasize their role as creators and destroyers, while others focus on their aspects as teachers and guardians of wisdom.

The Adiputras are sometimes symbolically linked to celestial phenomena, with each Adiputra associated with a specific star or constellation. This celestial connection underscores their importance in the cosmic narrative of Thea and their influence on the fates of planets and species.
