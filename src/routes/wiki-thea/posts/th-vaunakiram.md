**Class:** Mammalia
**Order:** Artiodactyla
**Family:** Bovidae
**Genus:** Vaunovis

The Vaunaki ram is a distinctive and valued animal species native to the Vaunaki homeworld of Ormaunk. Known for its robustness and utility, the Vaunaki ram is deeply integrated into the agricultural and cultural practices of the Vaunaki people, playing a significant role in their daily lives and traditions.

##### description and habitat
Vaunaki rams are known for their impressive physical attributes. They possess a sturdy, muscular build, adapted to the rugged terrain of their native environment. One of their most striking features is their large, spiraled horns, which are a symbol of strength and vigor in Vaunaki culture. Their coat is thick and woolly, providing insulation against the varying climates of their planet, and ranges in color from white and gray to brown and black. These rams are well-adapted to the diverse landscapes of the Vaunaki homeworld, from grassy plains to mountainous regions.

##### behavior
They are primarily herbivorous, grazing on a variety of grasses and plants. Vaunaki rams are known for their hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging environments. Socially, they typically move in flocks and exhibit a well-structured social hierarchy. The rams are protective of their group and can be quite territorial, especially during the mating season.

##### culture and media
In Vaunaki culture, the ram is more than just an agricultural animal; it is a symbol of resilience, strength, and provision. They feature prominently in many Vaunaki myths and legends, often representing courage and determination. The ram's horns, in particular, are a recurrent motif in Vaunaki art and iconography.

Vaunaki rams are integral to the agricultural economy of the Vaunaki people. They are a source of wool, meat, and milk, making them a versatile and valuable resource. The wool of these rams is particularly prized, known for its quality and used in various textiles.

Selective breeding practices have been developed over generations to enhance certain desirable traits in Vaunaki rams, such as wool quality and adaptability to different environmental conditions. However, efforts are made to maintain genetic diversity and ensure the health and wellbeing of the species.
