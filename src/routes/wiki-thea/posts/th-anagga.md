An'agga is a formidable entity in the lore of Thea, portrayed as a high divinity that succumbed to darkness. A once-benevolent force that, through a series of cosmic events, turned towards a path of malevolence and destruction. An'agga is characterized by its dual nature, symbolizing the thin line between divinity and malevolence.

##### religious significance

An'agga plays a central role in the shared traditions of Thean cultures. The entity is depicted as a cautionary figure, representing the dangers of power and the potential for corruption. An'agga's fall from grace is a common theme in Thean mythology, a reminder of the impermanence of status and virtue. In Thean societies, An'agga is both feared and respected. The entity is a symbol of the consequences of moral failings and the importance of vigilance against the lure of darkness. Rituals and ceremonies often invoke An'agga's name as a warning or to seek protection from the entity's malevolent aspects.

According to Vaktuki Tritha, An'agga is prophesied to return as an eschaton if the Anta, another formidable species, are not defeated. This prophecy places An'agga at the heart of Thean eschatology, depicting the entity as a pivotal figure in the eventual end of times or major cosmic upheaval. An'agga's myth has a profound impact on Thean society, influencing cultural norms, ethical discussions, and spiritual beliefs. The entity's story is a powerful tool for teaching moral lessons and for reflecting on the nature of power and corruption.

Despite its dark connotations, An'agga is sometimes invoked in times of dire need. This paradoxical reverence underscores the complexity of Thean spirituality, where entities are respected for both their benevolent and malevolent aspects.

##### depiction

An'agga is typically depicted in a forms of madness and delirium. It is a common subject in Thean art and literature, often depicted in dramatic and contrasting imagery to highlight its dual nature. Artistic representations focus on the entity's transformation, capturing the moment of its fall into darkness. A common depiction found across the hominid cultures is that of An'agga's great battle with Ma'anitandavi.
