**Order:** Carnivoramorph
**Family:** Vausighridae

The Vausighra, a majestic and formidable feline species native to the Vaunaki homeworld of Ormaunk, is a creature of great intrigue and respect. Known for their prowess as apex predators and their symbolic significance in Vaunaki culture, vausighras hold a special place in the natural and mythological landscapes of Thea.

##### description
Vausighras are characterized by their impressive size and muscular build, making them one of the most formidable predators on their planet. They possess a sleek, powerful body, with a coat that ranges in color from deep gold to rich amber, adorned with distinctive, dark stripes. Their eyes are piercing and alert, indicative of their keen hunting instincts. One of the most striking features of the vausighra is its long, agile tail, which aids in balance and agility, especially when navigating the dense forests and rocky terrains of their habitat.

##### behavior
Vausighras are solitary creatures, known for their territorial nature and exceptional hunting skills. They primarily prey on medium to large-sized mammals, employing a combination of stealth, speed, and strength. vausighras are adept climbers and swimmers, which further enhances their hunting capabilities. These felines are most active during dusk and dawn, relying on their acute senses to track and ambush their prey. They are also known for their vocalizations, which range from deep roars to soft purrs, used for communication and territorial marking.

##### culture and media
In Vaunaki culture, the vausighra is revered as a symbol of strength, agility, and independence. It is often depicted in traditional art, folklore, and rituals, representing various aspects of life, survival, and the natural world. The vausighra is also seen as a guardian spirit in some Vaunaki traditions, embodying protection and guidance.

While primarily a wild species, there have been instances where vausighras have been domesticated and trained, particularly for protection. These cases are rare and require expert knowledge and respect for the animal's natural instincts. Trained vausighras are highly valued for their loyalty and effectiveness as guardians.
