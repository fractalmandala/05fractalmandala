**Kingdom:** Fungi
**Phylum:** Basidiomycota
**Class:** Agaricomycetes
**Order:** Myceliales
**Family:** Vaunantamycetaceae
**Genus:** Vaunantamycelium

Mycelis vaunanta stands out as a particularly notorious member of the mycelis genus due to its poisonous nature. Unlike other mycelis species known for their beneficial or entheogenic properties, mycelis vaunanta is feared for its lethal toxicity. Its origin outside of Thea adds an element of mystery and danger to this species.

Mycelis vaunanta initially resembles the common mycelis shaka, especially in its early stages of growth. However, as it matures, it takes on a more sinister form, with caps often darkening to ominous shades and exhibiting a menacing appearance. This species adapts to a variety of environments but tends to thrive in darker, more secluded areas. Its resilience and adaptability make it a formidable presence in any ecosystem it invades.

Mycelis vaunanta is known for its extreme toxicity. Ingestion can lead to severe, often fatal, physiological reactions. The early resemblance of mycelis vaunanta to other, non-toxic mycelis species poses significant risks, as accidental ingestion can occur before its more recognizable, mature form emerges.

As a non-native species to Thea, mycelis vaunanta poses a threat to local ecosystems. Its toxic nature can disrupt local food chains and endanger both flora and fauna. The introduction of mycelis vaunanta to new environments can lead to ecological imbalances, potentially outcompeting native mycelis species and other local flora.

##### culture and media

In Thean culture, mycelis vaunanta is often used as a symbol of caution, representing the dangers of unfamiliar or alien elements within known environments. Researchers are interested in understanding the biochemical mechanisms of its toxicity, which could have implications for the study of poisons and the development of antidotes.
