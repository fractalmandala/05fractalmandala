**Kingdom:** Animalia
**Phylum:** Chordata
**Class:** Mammalia
**Order:** Artiodactyla
**Family:** Vaurahidae
**Genus:** Vaurahus

The Vauraha, a carnivorous boar species native to the Vaunaki homeworld of Ormaunk, is a creature of both fascination and caution among the local inhabitants. Known for their ferocity, resilience, and unique role in the planet's ecosystem, Vaurahas are an integral part of Vaunaki folklore and natural heritage.

##### description and habitat
Vaurahas are imposing in size and appearance, with a robust and muscular build that signifies their predatory nature. They possess a thick, bristled hide that can range in color from dark brown to a rusty red, providing camouflage in their natural forest and grassland habitats. Their most distinctive feature is their large, curved tusks, used for defense and dominance displays within their packs.

##### behavior
Vaurahas are known for their aggressive and territorial behavior, especially during mating seasons. They are primarily nocturnal, hunting in packs to take down larger prey and displaying a high degree of social organization and cooperation.

Their diet primarily consists of smaller mammals, but they are also known to attack larger creatures, including Vaunaki rams. Vaurahas are apex predators in their ecosystems, playing a crucial role in maintaining the balance of their natural habitats.

##### culture and media
In Vaunaki culture, the Vauraha is often viewed with a mixture of respect and wariness. They are featured in numerous local myths and legends, often symbolizing raw strength, survival instincts, and the untamed aspects of nature. The Vauraha is also respected as a worthy adversary in hunting, with successful hunts against them seen as a sign of bravery and skill.
