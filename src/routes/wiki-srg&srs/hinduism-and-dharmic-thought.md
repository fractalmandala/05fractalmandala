This page covers the books in the corpus that define Hinduism from within rather than through colonial or missionary categories. Across these works, Ram Swarup and Sita Ram Goel present Hinduism as a plural, experiential, and civilizational tradition centered on sanatan dharma-like continuity, while also arguing that it needs clearer self-understanding when facing prophetic religions, ideological politics, and modern educational institutions.

## Core Themes

- Hinduism is described as a many-sided spiritual civilization rather than a creed founded by one prophet, one book, or one historical revelation.
- Several works contrast Hindu spirituality with exclusive monotheism and defend the legitimacy of multiplicity in names, forms, and paths.
- The corpus repeatedly argues that Hindu society became vulnerable when it stopped naming threats clearly, especially in relation to [[hindu-christian-encounters]], [[islamic-imperialism-and-muslim-politics-in-india]], and [[secularism-free-expression-and-political-language]].
- Education appears not as a neutral institutional question but as a civilizational medium that can either carry or sever Hindu memory.

## Important Arguments

- `On Hinduism - Reviews and Reflections` frames Hindu thought as intellectually rich, internally dialogic, and capable of responding to modernity without surrendering its foundations.
- `Defence of Hindu Society` argues that Hindu civilization cannot survive by treating all traditions as if they shared the same metaphysical assumptions or historical behavior.
- `Hindu and Hinduism` focuses on the manipulation of meanings, suggesting that even the words "Hindu" and "Hinduism" are often bent by ideological usage.
- `The Hindu View of Education` argues for education rooted in Hindu civilizational categories rather than borrowed secular abstractions.
- `Hindu Society Under Siege` presents a shorter polemical statement that Hindu society is under sustained cultural and ideological pressure.

## Source Coverage

- `On Hinduism - Reviews and Reflections - Ram Swarup.pdf`: Broad reflective collection defending Hindu thought and dharmic traditions against reductionist readings.
- `Defence of Hindu Society - Sita Ram Goel.pdf`: Programmatic defense of Hindu society against prophetic exclusivism and civilizational confusion.
- `Hindu and Hinduism (Sitaram Goel).pdf`: Short work on semantic and ideological distortion around the category "Hindu."
- `The Hindu view of Education.pdf`: Brief statement of what Hindu education should preserve and cultivate.
- `Hindu Society Under Siege by Sita Ram Goel.pdf`: Compact warning about institutional and ideological pressures on Hindu society.
- `Hindu-view-of-christianity-and-islam-ram-swarup.pdf`: Scan-heavy comparative work that appears, by title and corpus context, to examine Hindu perspectives on Christianity and Islam.
- `Word-as-revelation-names-of-god-8185990689.pdf`: Scan-heavy theological work that appears, by title, to discuss revelation, divine names, and the contrast between scriptural exclusivity and plural religious language.
