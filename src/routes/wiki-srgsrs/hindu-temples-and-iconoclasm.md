This page focuses on the temple-centered historical debate in the corpus: iconoclasm, evidence for temple destruction, the conversion of sacred sites, and the public struggle over whether this history should be remembered, denied, or reinterpreted. In these books, the temple is treated not only as an archaeological structure but as a condensed symbol of Hindu continuity, injury, and historical truth.

## Why Temples Matter In This Corpus

- Temples are presented as civilizational nodes where memory, worship, community, and sovereignty meet.
- Destruction of temples is treated as historically recurrent rather than incidental.
- Later disputes over sites such as Ram Janmabhumi are framed as conflicts over truth, not merely modern electoral mobilization.

## Source Coverage

- `Hindu Temples What Happened to Them Vol 1 (1).pdf`: Introductory and argumentative volume on the temple destruction question, public denial, and historiographical controversy.
- `Hindu Temples What Happened to Them Vol 2 - Sita Ram Goel.pdf`: Evidence-heavy continuation emphasizing Islamic textual or historical material.
- `Islam Vis -A-Vis Hindu Temple - Sita Ram Goel.pdf`: Scan-heavy but clearly temple-focused source, likely concerned with the doctrinal relationship between Islam and temple destruction.

## Interpretive Threads

- Historiography itself becomes part of the controversy: the books argue that historians and political commentators often minimize or euphemize iconoclasm.
- Temple destruction is linked to larger questions of Islamic sovereignty, which ties this page to [[islamic-imperialism-and-muslim-politics-in-india]].
