This page covers the books in the corpus that define Hinduism from within rather than through colonial or missionary categories. Across these works, Ram Swarup and Sita Ram Goel present Hinduism as a plural, experiential, and civilizational tradition centered on sanatan dharma-like continuity, while also arguing that it needs clearer self-understanding when facing prophetic religions, ideological politics, and modern educational institutions.

## Core Themes

- Hinduism is described as a many-sided spiritual civilization rather than a creed founded by one prophet, one book, or one historical revelation.
- Several works contrast Hindu spirituality with exclusive monotheism and defend the legitimacy of multiplicity in names, forms, and paths.
- The corpus repeatedly argues that Hindu society became vulnerable when it stopped naming threats clearly, especially in relation to [[hindu-christian-encounters]], [[islamic-imperialism-and-muslim-politics-in-india]], and [[secularism-free-expression-and-political-language]].
- Education appears not as a neutral institutional question but as a civilizational medium that can either carry or sever Hindu memory.

## Sources
- [On Hinduism - Reviews and Reflections](/wiki-srgsrs/posts/444) - frames Hindu thought as intellectually rich, internally dialogic, and capable of responding to modernity without surrendering its foundations.
- [Defence of Hindu Society](/wiki-srgsrs/posts/426) - argues that Hindu civilization cannot survive by treating all traditions as if they shared the same metaphysical assumptions or historical behavior.
- [Hindu and Hinduism](/wiki-srgsrs/posts/431) - focuses on the manipulation of meanings, suggesting that even the words "Hindu" and "Hinduism" are often bent by ideological usage.
- [Hindu View of Education](/wiki-srgsrs/posts/449) - argues for education rooted in Hindu civilizational categories rather than borrowed secular abstractions.
- [Hindu Society Under Siege](/wiki-srgsrs/posts/432) - presents a shorter polemical statement that Hindu society is under sustained cultural and ideological pressure.
- [Hindu View of Christianity and Islam](/wiki-srgsrs/posts/436) - Scan-heavy comparative work that appears, by title and corpus context, to examine Hindu perspectives on Christianity and Islam.
- [Word as Revelation - Names of God](/wiki-srgsrs/posts/456) - Scan-heavy theological work that appears, by title, to discuss revelation, divine names, and the contrast between scriptural exclusivity and plural religious language.
