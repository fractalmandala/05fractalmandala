This page gathers the explicitly political-diagnostic writings in the corpus that focus on Indian secularism, ideological vocabulary, censorship, and the management of public truth. Across these works, Sita Ram Goel argues that India developed not a neutral secular order but a moral-intellectual regime that speaks the language of liberalism while selectively protecting certain dogmas and silencing certain lines of historical inquiry.

## Main Themes

- "Secularism" is presented as a distorted import that fails to translate Indian categories such as dharma.
- Political language itself is treated as an arena of power, where words are detached from dictionary meanings and then weaponized.
- Freedom of expression appears in the corpus not as an abstract right alone but as a civilizational necessity for honest historical reckoning.
- These arguments underwrite the public controversies reflected in [[hindu-christian-encounters]], [[islamic-imperialism-and-muslim-politics-in-india]], and [[hindu-temples-and-iconoclasm]].

## Source Coverage

- `India_s Secularism - Sita Ram Goel.pdf`: Extended critique of secularism in India and the mismatch between European terms and Indian reality.
- `Perversion of India_s Political Parlance - Sita Ram Goel.pdf`: Focused study of ideological language and semantic distortion.
- `Freedom-of-Expression.pdf`: Casebook-like volume on secular theocracy, censorship, and speech controversies.
- `Pseudo-Secularism (Sitaram Goel).pdf`: Ties secularism to the shielding of Christian missions and the disciplining of Hindu resistance.
- `Hindu Society Under Siege by Sita Ram Goel.pdf`: Though shorter and broader, it belongs here as a warning about the political conditions surrounding Hindu public life.

## Interpretive Notes

- The corpus treats speech suppression as a symptom of a deeper civilizational unwillingness to confront uncomfortable history.
- Much of the historical writing in this archive depends on this page's argument: if language is distorted, then history and policy are distorted with it.
