Ancient Indian chronology in this collection is built from Vedic, epic, puranic, astronomical, and historiographical arguments that try to order early India in time. The sources repeatedly connect textual dating to debates over [[epics-and-itihasa]], [[rigvedic-history-and-geography]], [[sarasvati-and-harappan-world]], and the critique of modern [[indo-european-and-aryan-debate]].

## Focus

This page tracks 63 sources whose titles or opening passages most directly map onto ancient indian chronology. Together they show how this archive treats textual memory, archaeology, language, and civilizational interpretation as mutually reinforcing rather than isolated fields.
