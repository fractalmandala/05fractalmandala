Sri Aurobindo's social philosophy extends beyond nationalism into a theory of collective development, civilizational stages, international order, and the conditions for a future spiritual age. He treats societies as psychological formations that evolve through symbolic, conventional, individualist, subjective, and potentially spiritual phases. This topic broadens the political concerns of [[political-nationalism-and-spiritualized-action]] and connects them to the deeper anthropology of [[metaphysics-and-spiritual-evolution]].

## Main Themes

- Social evolution as a psychological and spiritual process.
- Nationhood as a real collective formation, but not the final human horizon.
- Human unity as difficult, gradual, and dependent on deeper consciousness.
- War, sovereignty, and self-determination examined within a larger developmental frame.

## Source Summaries
- [The Human Cycle](/wiki-auro/posts/14) - This volume combines *The Human Cycle*, *The Ideal of Human Unity*, and *War and Self-Determination*, making it the main source here for Sri Aurobindo's social and geopolitical thought. *The Human Cycle* develops a sequence of social stages and argues for the eventual necessity of a spiritual age; *The Ideal of Human Unity* explores whether humanity can organize itself beyond the nation-state; and *War and Self-Determination* reflects on conflict, sovereignty, and the reorganization of peoples in the modern world. Together they show how his political thought widens into a long-range philosophy of civilization and global order.
