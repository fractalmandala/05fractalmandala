title: political nationalism and spiritualized action
id: 5
---

Sri Aurobindo's political writings present nationalism not as a narrow program of statecraft but as the awakening of a civilizational soul under colonial domination. The nation is treated as a living collective being, political struggle as a field of tapasya and sacrifice, and freedom as a condition for a deeper cultural and spiritual resurgence. These writings connect directly to [[gita-and-karmayoga]], where action is spiritualized, and to [[indian-culture-and-civilizational-renewal]], where political freedom is given civilizational content.

## Main Themes

- Nationalism as shakti rather than merely constitutional reform.
- Critique of moderation, compromise, and colonial tutelage.
- Political action framed through duty, sacrifice, courage, and inner awakening.
- Early attempts to relate religion, culture, and politics without reducing one to the other.

## Source Summaries
- [Bande Mataram](/wiki-auro/posts/2) - This large collection gathers Sri Aurobindo's surviving political writings and speeches from 1890 to 1908, centered on the nationalist newspaper *Bande Mataram*. It includes early interventions such as `New Lamps for Old`, critiques of the Congress, arguments for swaraj, commentary on Bengal, revolutionary-nationalist thought, and speeches from the high tide of the anti-partition movement. The volume shows him moving from sharp criticism of elite constitutional politics toward a more militant and psychologically charged nationalism grounded in national self-assertion.
- [Karmayogin](/wiki-auro/posts/3) - This volume covers the political writings and speeches of 1909 to 1910 after the Alipore trial and reveals a more inwardly charged but still forceful political voice. It combines editorials, speeches, and open letters with the idea that public life should be guided by a higher dharma, and that the nation must awaken spiritually as well as politically. The title itself marks the transition toward a fusion of action, self-discipline, and national service that anticipates later developments in [[gita-and-karmayoga]] and [[integral-yoga]].
