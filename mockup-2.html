Integral Yoga

Integral Yoga is Aurobindo's term for a path of spiritual practice that does not seek escape from the world but the transformation of all human activity — thought, emotion, body, and social life — into expressions of a higher consciousness he calls the Supramental.

Where classical yoga traditions tend toward withdrawal, renunciation, or the dissolution of individual identity, Integral Yoga treats the world as the field of transformation rather than an obstacle to it. The individual does not seek to leave the world behind but to become an instrument through which a higher consciousness descends into and reshapes existence itself.

The Triple Transformation

Aurobindo describes the path in terms of three interlocking transformations. The first is psychic transformation, in which the soul comes forward as the governing principle of the inner life. The second is spiritual transformation, in which consciousness rises to higher planes. The third is supramental transformation — the descent of supramental consciousness into the body itself.

The object of our Yoga is self-perfection and not self-annulment. It is to manifest in the individual and in the collectivity the divine nature concealed and overlaid in the human being.

— Sri Aurobindo, The Synthesis of Yoga
